A Tapestry of History and Culture:

Galle city invites you to immerse yourself in its rich tapestry of history, culture, and natural beauty. Wander through the cobbled streets of the iconic Galle Fort, where centuries-old Dutch-colonial buildings stand as a testament to the city's colonial past. Explore the vibrant markets, artisanal shops, and quaint cafes nestled within the fort's walls, each offering a glimpse into Galle's unique charm.

Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery: A Sanctuary for Endangered Species:

ust a short distance from Galle lies the coastal village of Kosgoda, home to a remarkable turtle hatchery dedicated to the conservation of these magnificent creatures. Witness firsthand the efforts to protect and nurture endangered turtle species as you tour the hatchery and learn about the lifecycle of these ancient reptiles. Hold a baby turtle in your hands and release it into the ocean, knowing that your visit contributes to their preservation.

Madu River: Nature's Serene Playground:

Embark on a boat journey along the enchanting Madu River, where mangrove forests, exotic wildlife, and traditional fishing villages paint a picture of untouched natural beauty. Glide past verdant landscapes and serene waterscapes, stopping to explore mystical islands and hidden temples along the way. Feel the gentle breeze caress your skin as you soak in the tranquility of this idyllic riverine paradise.

Experience the magic of Galle, Kosgoda, and the Madu River, where every moment is a celebration of Sri Lanka's diverse and enchanting landscapes.
